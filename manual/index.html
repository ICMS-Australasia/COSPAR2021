Custom Stands

For Sponsors planning a custom booth build, please refer to the important information below.

All build plans must be sighted by the ICC Sydney. The build plans will be checked for operational servicing only and not as to whether it meets and complies with all or any relevant regulations and building codes. It is the responsibility of the customer to forward accurate details to the venue. All build plans should not be considered final until sighted by the ICC Sydney.

Please note: your organisation will be responsible for the cost of the booth construction.

Please see below guidelines from ICC Sydney:

  • No structure can be fixed to the venue structure and must not interfere with adjacent booths.
  • No equipment, fittings or materials may be placed in any aisle walkway or in such a position that the access to any designated exit is in any way obstructed.
  • Materials used in booth construction and design must not be readily ignitable nor emit toxic fumes should ignition take place.
  • The structure of the building must remain in the same condition as prior to set-up. Any changes to the building and property of the ICC Sydney will be charged to the company at fault.
  • Any heavy equipment display must be approved before display. Detailed information on dimension and weight to be provided. Please email these through with your custom booth plans.
  • The exhibitor is required to provide the detailed plan
  • Any double storey booth as part of the Engineer Certificate will clearly define the number of occupants allowed taking into account any other item on the second story.
  • Booth dimensions fit the allocated space with sufficient clearance.
  • Materials used for booth construction comply with fire hazard properties set out in the National Construction Code 2016.
  • All materials used for lining, drapes, backdrops, blinds or overhead structures, signs, banners, theming or similar for displays or any part available to the public are to be fire retardant as per the National Construction Code 2016 requirements and other relevant Australian Standards.
  • ICC Sydney will request submission of all information or documentation confirming the retardant capabilities of the materials.
  • Use materials that do not cause dampness, stain, not readily ignitable or capable of emitting toxic fumes in case of ignition.
  • All two storey custom booths will require an engineer’s certification on completion.
  • Plywood, hardwood, pulp board or fibreboard are to be rendered flame-resistant by a process deemed acceptable by fire authorities.
  • When decorating or theming, materials such as crepe paper, corrugated cardboard, straw, hay bales, untreated hessian or PVC sheet (except on floors as a protective membrane) are not to be used without prior ICC Sydney approval.
  • Sawdust, tan bark or wood chips of a reasonable size may be used to decorate floors, provided a protective membrane is laid first and chips are kept slightly moist at all times. Cleaning charges will apply.
  • Any custom-built booth with a floor area of more than fifty (50) square metres is to be provided with at least one (1) alternative means of egress to the walkways.
  • Ensure all raised floors with a height of less than 115mm have a clearly distinguishable ramped edge.
  • To prevent trip hazards on standard 32mm high raised floors, ramped edging with a gradient no less than 1:3 is generally acceptable in the exhibition industry; however, please note that a 1:3 ramped edge does not constitute a disabled access ramp. For raised floors above 32mm and below 115mm it is recommended that the gradient of the ramped edging be decreased to 1:8.
  • All ramped edges are to be contained within the contracted space.
  • Maximum build height in The Gallery is 4m high.

Transport and access options

Airport

Darling Harbour is located only 8km from Sydney Airport, with an average drive time of 25 minutes.

Bus

ICC Sydney has its own bus stop. Bus routes 389 and 501 stop at Harris Street near Allen Street - a 10-minute walk from ICC Sydney.

Ferry

Cruise into Darling Harbour on a ferry with direct services from Circular Quay, King Street Wharf or Pyrmont Bay Wharf. Captain Cook Cruises operates services between Darling Harbour Convention Wharf, Barangaroo King Street Wharf No. 1 and Circular Quay.

Sydney Light Rail

The Light Rail travels right through a number of Darling Harbour stations including ICC Sydney’s stops, Convention Centre and Exhibition Centre. The Light Rail starts at Central Station in the CBD and travels to Dulwich Hill in the inner west.

Taxi

The best taxi drop off and pick up locations for events hosted at the ICC Sydney Convention Centre is the new Iron Wharf Place next to Harbourside Shopping Centre.

Train

A 10-minute walk from Central or Town Hall Stations will take you directly to ICC Sydney as well as Darling Quarter, Tumbalong Park and Sydney Visitor Centre.

Water Taxi

A water taxi is a great option for getting to and from Darling Harbour. Water taxis depart from The Rocks, Circular Quay, Opera House and Luna Park.

Walking

Sydney is a walkable city - routes to the venue include from Central Station via The Goods Line.

Opal Card

Opal makes getting around on public transport easy. Opal cards are smartcard tickets that you keep, reload and reuse to pay for travel on public transport. The Opal network includes train, bus, ferry and light rail services in Sydney, the Blue Mountains, Central Coast, Hunter, Illawarra and Southern Highlands.

If you have an American Express, Mastercard or Visa credit or debit card or a linked device, you can use it to pay for your travel by tapping on and tapping off at Opal readers. Just look for the contactless payment symbol . Contactless payments are available on all public transport in the Opal network.

Freight Information

The ICC Loading Dock is managed by the ICC team. Deliveries of any description (including exhibitor goods) must be sent through the preferred freight forwarder or courier.

Goods must not be sent to the Loading Dock before the scheduled times for the move-in of your event. All goods must be collected by the end of the official move-out time.

ICC Sydney will not sign possession of goods from couriers on behalf of exhibitors. Please ensure you or a representative is on site to accept and sign for deliveries. ICC Sydney takes no responsibility for unattended goods on site and will not sign for exhibitor deliveries; exhibitors are solely responsible for the safety or well-being of any items on, or delivered to, the site if unable to be present and accept deliveries or assign a contractor to do so. Deliveries not through the preferred freight forwarder must be delivered on the exhibitor move in day.

ICC Sydney does not have venue storage and is therefore unable to accept freight shipments before schedule move in times. Freight consignment and off-site storage arrangements before, during and after the hiring period are the responsibilities of the event organiser.

  • weight, dimension, make, model and/or data sheet of items to be rigged
  • plans, elevations and/or layouts detailing the design of items to be rigged.

If you plan to include rigging in your custom booth build, please include this information in your custom booth drawings, complete the ICC Sydney Banner Hanging and Rigging Form and send through for approval to kaylab@icmsaust.com.au before 06 November 2020.

Will packing supplies be provided?

Please note the organiser does not supply any of the below materials. It is recommended that all exhibitors come prepared with the following:

  • Courier details
  • Prepared freight labels for return of goods
  • Stationery
  • Packing tape
  • Velcro, blu-tack and other adhesives

What can I have on my exhibition booth?

We encourage you to be creative with the items that you have on your exhibition booth as this will assist in encouraging delegate traffic to your display.

Please note the below items are sponsored items within the COSPAR 2021 Partnership and Marketing Opportunities Brochure and therefore are not allowed on exhibition booths or displays within the exhibition:

  • Barista/Coffee machines
  • Ice Cream Station
  • Juice/Smoothie Station

If your exhibition booth is sampling food or having food giveaways, you will be required to complete a Food and Beverage Sampling Form and submit this to ICC Sydney.

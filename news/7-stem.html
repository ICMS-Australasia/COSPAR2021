Australia is buzzing with the space conversation at the moment. With a new space agency, a huge research push and many new space start-ups, there are more opportunities than ever in Australia to be part of the journey. We are also facing new and complex global challenges brought on by climate change and population growth.

Young people embrace complexity and the generation of new ideas and they care about these challenges and opportunities we face on Earth. This is exactly the opportunity that space brings to Australia and to the globe, to not just inspire the scientists and engineers, but the thinkers and leaders of the future that we need.

Next year during National Science week, we will be making a big step in that direction with the world's largest space research conference being held in Sydney, COSPAR 2020.

Alongside the research conversations is COSPAR-K, the dedicated STEM Park featuring interactive experiences and activations for Australian students, using space to ignite passion in our future thinkers and leaders.

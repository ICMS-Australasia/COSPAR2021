GIRLS WE NEED YOU AT COSPAR-K

17 – 19 August 2020
ICC Sydney

Did you know, 539 trips have been made into space? The first woman sent to space was Valentina Tereshkova in 1963, just two years after her male counterpart. We waited almost 20 years for the next woman to make this feat. Despite making up almost 50% of the world’s population, women attribute to less than 11% of history’s space explorers.

Recently, there has been an increased push to inspire and engage women and girls in sciences. Women and girls continue to be underrepresented with long-standing biases and gender stereotypes steering girls and women away from science-related professions.

Today on the International Day of Women & Girls in Science, we want to fix this statistic and call upon women and girls to consider a future in space science.

The International Day of Women and Girls in Science is implemented by UNESCO and UN-Women as an opportunity to promote full and equal access to and participation in science for women and girls. Gender equality is a global priority for UNESCO and the support of young girls, their education and their full ability to make their ideas heard are levers for development and peace. Diversity in research expands the pool of brilliant researchers, bringing in fresh perspectives, talent and creativity.

This year during National Science Week, we will be making a big step in the right direction with the world's largest space research conference being held in Sydney, COSPAR 2020.

Alongside the research conversations is COSPAR-K, the dedicated STEM Park featuring interactive experiences and activations for students, using space to ignite passion in our future thinkers and leaders.
